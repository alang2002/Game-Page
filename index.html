My Favorite Gaming Systems


Microsoft's Xbox One

The Xbox One was made by Microsoft. It was released in 2013 and is one of the next gen consoles we know today. It's largest competitor is the PS4 from Sony. Since it's release, several editions of the popular console have been put on the market, like the Xbox One S.



Sony's Playstation 4

The Playstation 4 was made by Sony. It was released in 2013 as a competitor to the also successful Xbox One. It is the eight generation of home consoles. It has a large amount of exclusive titles compared to the Xbox One. It also features other editions, like the PS4 Pro.



Nintendo's Nintendo Switch

The Nintendo Switch is one of the first major consoles to feature a sort of "on the go" idea. It has a screen built into the console itself, along with motion controllers, called "Joycons", similar to how the Wii controller works. Many have claimed it to be quite innovative as no other major console on the market is capable of this.



Valve's HTC Vive

The HTC Vive is a VR (virtual reality) headset developed by both HTC and Valve. It uses a headset that is mounted to the user's upper face, acting like a giant pair of goggles. It immerses the user in a virtual world by using motion tracking, and handheld controllers shaped to fit naturally within the user's hand.



Gaming PC

Aside from home consoles, there are gaming computers. These are home computers that are suited mainly for gaming rather than work or personal use. Most usually come with plenty of RAM, storage, a good graphics card and CPU. Each part is just as important as the other, and they almost always cost hundreds of dollars, usually over $1000 or above. High end PCs sometimes go over $2000. Overall though, they are more capable than home consoles, able to achieve higher frames per second, play more titles, and among other things.
